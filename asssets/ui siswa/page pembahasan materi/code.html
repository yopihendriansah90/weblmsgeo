Understanding Qubits and Superposition

In traditional computing, a bit is the smallest unit of data, existing as either a 0 or a 1. In quantum computing, we use quantum bits, or **qubits**. Unlike a standard bit, a qubit can exist in multiple states at once through a phenomenon called superposition.

Key Concepts to Remember

  • bolt
    Superposition

    The ability of a quantum system to be in multiple states at the same time until it is measured.

  • join_inner
    Entanglement

    A physical phenomenon where pairs or groups of particles are generated or interact in ways such that the quantum state of each particle cannot be described independently.
